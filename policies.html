Policies

  • Only vehicles with the current school year’s vehicle pass are allowed to enter the campus.
  • The vehicle pass is issued only to enrolled students, employees (includes full-time, part-time, contractual employees), POLCA officers, DLSAAI Officers, and third-party providers/ partners (e.g., concessionaires, suppliers of the concessionaires, transport service providers, etc.)
  • Only vehicles registered under the name of the applicant, or his/her spouse, or his/her daughter/son, or his/her parents may be issued a vehicle pass.
  • A vehicle pass cannot be used as a substitute for identification (ID) card.
  • Issuance of a vehicle pass is on a FIRST-COME-FIRST-SERVED BASIS and each applicant can apply for two (2) vehicles only. The price of vehicle pass depends on the category of application and includes Ecosystem service fee applicable to all vehicle entry pass applications equivalent to 20% of the vehicle pass cost.
  • Payment are highly encouraged to be made thru online payment gateway BUx-DLSUD and/or thru accredited banks and payment centers. Please take note that there may have additional 3% bank charge if you pay using credit card/debit cards online.
  • Public utility vehicles like jeepneys, taxicabs, tricycles or all those with yellow car plates are not allowed to enter the campus thus need not apply for vehicle pass.
  • Government-owned vehicles (with red plate) shall not be issued a vehicle pass.
  • Motorcycle drivers are required to wear their helmet and remove them upon entry at the gate for identification purposes. Back rides shall be allowed following IATF guidelines.
  • A vehicle pass is valid for one (1) school year only. In case that the student shall not enroll in the 2nd semester of the school year, the vehicle sticker shall be automatically cancelled. In case of Leave of Absence (LOA) same procedure shall apply.
  • The maximum speed limit of 20 kph shall be observed at all times to all types of vehicles including motorcycle. For students, please refer to student handbook Section 19.3.1.G.
  • The security is authorized to inspect the vehicle’s compartment and under chassis upon entry and exit.
  • Vehicles shall be parked at the designated parking areas on a first-come-first-served basis and parked vehicles are not allowed to run engine on idle for more than 3 minutes.
  • Road signs shall be strictly followed. No blowing of horns and no obstruction while inside the campus.
  • The University is not liable for any damage or loss of the vehicle or its contents, including but not limited to; force majeure, vehicular accident/collision, deliberately caused damage done by another person.
  • Overnight parking is not allowed.
  • Heavily tinted vehicles, smoke belchers, lowered (less than 6.5” ground clearance) and those with loud mufflers shall not be issued a vehicle pass.
  • For students, sanction for any infraction of this policy or any regulation on traffic management shall be covered by the Students’ Handbook. However, this does not prevent the General Services Office from cancelling previously issued vehicle pass or the non-renewal thereof for grounds of breach of road safety and security protocols.
  • For employees, POLCA officers, concessionaires and suppliers of the concessionaires, Third-party providers, and others, sanction for any infraction of this policy shall be cancellation of vehicle pass or non-renewal of the same.
  • The General Services Office (GSO) takes charge of screening applications, approval, issuance, and control of vehicle pass.
  • All vehicle pass applications shall be processed at GSO upon completion of the following requirements:
    1. Valid OR and CR of the Vehicle;
    2. Certificate of Registration (Enrollment for AY 2022-2023);
    3. Certification from matron if applicant is a dorm occupant;
    4. Certification from MRO Head if applicant is a concessionaire or supplier of the concessionaire;
    5. Certification from the Office Head, who is served for Third-Party providers;
    6. For SHS students who will be driving their own vehicles shall secure an endorsement/approval slip from the Prefect Office;
    7. For those using company vehicle, submit certification from the company that the vehicle is assigned to the qrInfo:
  • Third Party providers and their employees and suppliers shall be required to submit a certification of road worthiness from the Transportation Office as part of the requirements.
  • Vehicle pass of sold vehicles should be surrendered to the GSO – before application for another one.
  • An applicant whose vehicle did not pass the security inspection shall be entitled to a refund.
  • Guidelines on traffic rules and regulations should be strictly followed.
